Final Insights
Project Summary

This project analyzes a cleaned sample of NYC 311 service requests from 2024 using Python, SQLite, and SQL.

The goal was to identify complaint patterns, compare borough performance, and measure how long different types of requests take to resolve.

Dataset Summary
Records analyzed: 59,419
Complaint types: 148
Agencies: 14
Time period: 2024

Key Findings:
1. Brooklyn received the most complaints.

Brooklyn accounted for about 32% of all service requests in the dataset.

2. Illegal Parking was the most common complaint.

Illegal Parking made up about 16% of all complaints and was the most common complaint in four of the five boroughs.

The Bronx was the only borough where HEAT/HOT WATER was the most common complaint.

3. Resolution times varied across boroughs.

Staten Island had the longest average resolution time, while the Bronx had the shortest.

4. Some complaint types consistently took longer to resolve.

Examples include:

New Tree Request
Mobile Food Vendor
Building/Use
Food Establishment

These requests often involve inspections or field work, which may explain the longer resolution times.

5. Complaint volume remained consistent throughout the year.

The monthly sample contained roughly 5,000 requests each month, with no major changes in complaint volume.

Conclusion

Using Python for data preparation and SQL for analysis made it possible to explore complaint trends and identify differences in service performance across New York City.
